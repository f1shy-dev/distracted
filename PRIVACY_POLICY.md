Privacy Policy for "distracted" Browser Extension

Last Updated: 27/12/2025

OVERVIEW

distracted is a browser extension that blocks distracting websites by requiring brief challenges before access. This policy explains data handling.

DATA COLLECTION

Stored locally on your device:

1. Blocked site configuration: Website URLs/patterns you configure, challenge settings, auto-relock preferences
2. Optional statistics (if enabled): Visit count, challenge completion count, time spent on sites after unlocking
3. Extension settings: Whether statistics tracking is enabled

We do NOT collect: Personally identifiable information, health information, financial information, authentication credentials, personal communications, location data, complete browsing history, data from websites you visit.

DATA STORAGE AND TRANSMISSION

All data stored locally using browser storage APIs. No data transmitted to external servers or third parties. No analytics or tracking services. No network requests to external servers.

DATA ACCESS AND CONTROL

You control your data: Disable statistics through settings, clear statistics through settings, delete blocked sites anytime, uninstalling removes all data.

DATA RETENTION

Data retained until you delete it through settings, clear browser extension storage, or uninstall the extension.

THIRD-PARTY SERVICES

No third-party services, analytics, or tracking tools used.

CHANGES TO POLICY

Policy may be updated. Last Updated date indicates changes. Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance.

CONTACT

Questions about privacy policy or data handling:
GitHub Issues: https://github.com/f1shy-dev/distracted/issues

COMPLIANCE

Complies with Chrome Web Store Developer Program Policies, GDPR principles (data minimization, local storage only), and browser extension privacy best practices.

SUMMARY

Your data stays on your device. We don't collect, transmit, or share your information with anyone.
